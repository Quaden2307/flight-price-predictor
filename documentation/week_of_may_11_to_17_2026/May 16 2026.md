Spent most of the day on EDA in 03_live_data_visualization.ipynb. Two major plots and a bunch of pandas fundamentals.

Plot 5 — price vs distance scatter — landed cleanly. Clear upward slope, three visible distance clusters (short / medium / long), sublinear shape (a 14,000 km flight is ~4-5x more expensive than a 1,000 km one, not 14x). Distance is going to be one of the most important features in the eventual model. Modeling implications noted in plots_observations_phase2.

Plot 6 — 2x2 grid of price vs lead_time scatter, faceted by distance bucket. The hypothesis was that controlling for distance would reveal a hidden lead_time slope that was being masked by the bimodal distribution. Mostly didn't pan out — short and medium buckets look flat, only long maybe shows a faint downward slope. Realized I was probably reading a density artifact as a "trend" in my initial look, since dense low-lead-time regions visually appear higher in price.

Plot 7 — built a median-line plot to test the lead_time hypothesis properly. Group by (10-day lead bin, distance bucket), take median price within each group, plot median lines. Conclusion: long-haul has a real (if weak) downward slope, short and medium are flat. The 200-day spike in the short bucket is outliers + sparse data, not signal. Lead_time matters for long-haul flights, not for short or medium. Modeling implication: needs to be a distance × lead_time interaction in any linear model; tree models will handle this automatically.
